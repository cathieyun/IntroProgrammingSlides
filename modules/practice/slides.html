Short Lecture: Passwords

  • "Logging in" is just proving your identity to a website.
  • Send your "proof" (password) to the website's server, it will check database
  • Most simple model: send plain text login information to server.
  • What could go wrong?

What could go wrong?

  • What if the data (plain text login information) is intercepted?
  • What if the database is stolen?
  • Solution: hashing!

Hashing

  • Hashing takes in a string, outputs "nonsense" (hash).
  • One string will always have the same hash.
  • If you have a hash, you cannot easily guess the string.
  • Instead of sending and storing plain text, send and store hashes.
  • What could go wrong here?

Salting

  • What if your database gets stolen?
  • People have really bad passwords!
  • You can pre-hash the most common passwords and check them against database values.
  • This is called a Rainbow Table attack.
  • Solution: Add salts! A random number that is stored with the password.

How does Salting work?

  • A Rainbow Table attack will no longer work. (Explain why!)
  • How could you still find peoples' passwords, if you have the database?
  • Lesson: nothing is ever FULLY, PERFECTLY secure! We just have to do our best.

Review: Functions

  • Functions are just actions.
  • You can define your own functions, or use pre-defined functions.
  • In javascript, functions are values, just like strings and numbers!
  • Define your own function with the "var" and "function" keywords.
  • Whatever goes in the parenthesis of "function()" are the inputs to the function.

Review: For loops
